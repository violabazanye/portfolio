Chef Bot

Transforming the ordinary recipe app into a more engaging and intellectual application that uses artificial intelligence, and provides quick results tailored to your needs. Check out the prototype.

The Problem

Given my love for creativity and also being a foodie, I like to bring a spontaneous element to every dish I prepare, and because of that, I found myself downloading several recipe apps on my phone to get as many ideas as I could. This would also be paired with some searching on Google as well. So you can imagine the level of cognitive overload I was faced with. But that's just me.
Eventually I decided to cut down on my hoarding of recipe apps and just pick one that was best. This didn't work out so well because almost every recipe app I have used is individually rich with information. When it is almost dinner time you would find me scrolling through tonnes of options that never come to an end, and after all of that, I would still have no idea on what to have for dinner.

Hence, a problem emerged: how can I redesign a recipe app into one that will not make users think, but will help them decide quickly and easily on what they can cook with the ingredients they have.

Users & Audience

The target users of Chef Bot are smartphone users with the ability and time to cook their own meals at home, also looking for new tasty and healthy recipes.
They are amateur chefs and need help deciding what to cook with the current ingredients they have, they want suggestions fast (since in some cases they may be super hungry) and tailored to their dietary preferences.

Currently, they are completing these tasks with the help of:

  • Google Search
  • Browsing through various existing recipe apps
  • Consulting friends
  • Some dare to experiment on their own (a.k.a. the lone ranger)

Team & Role

This being my first solo project, I had a core team of one - just me. Having had experience designing and developing mobile applications for startups, I got along pretty well.

I did all the work myself; conversation design, user task flows, interaction design, visual design, prototyping and development. I also conducted user research methods such as interviews and 'Wizard of Oz' experiments in order to assess both user behavior and attitudes.

Design Process

Since I was doing this project on my own, I decided to do things a little differently. First, I thought, with the current trend in technology, what are the current solutions available that can help enhance usabilty and reduce cognitive load ? That was when I landed on artificial intelligence (AI), which led me to conversational UIs.
Having a conversational UI gives the advantage of interacting with the application on human terms unlike just clicking on icons. This way the user has complete control on what the application can do. The intelligence doesn't come from words being recognized but from natural-language understanding of intentions behind those words.
So then I sketched what the MVP (minimum viable product) interface would look like.

The promises of AI powered interfaces are great but so are the challenges, for example with conversational UIs the main problems include:

  • understanding the intent of the user
  • understanding the context

With AI we must design for context and intent. The design requirements are very different and the main challenge for the interaction design of this application was to figure out what information is relevant in a given context and ignore the rest. My hope is that with AI, the app will eventually learn and make these kinds of judgements on its own.

Conversation Design

That said, I found Google's guidelines on conversational design to be extremely helpful in building a meaningful conversation. With this, I started by understanding, from interviews, who are my users.
First, what are some of the common words and phrases used;

  • I have chicken in the fridge, what can I cook with it?
  • I'm hungry but what can I eat?
  • How do they cook lasagna?
  • Beef recipes

Also, what are some of the common triggers for users to start thinking about these phrases;

  • hunger
  • cravings
  • if I have something in the fridge about to expire and I want to use it
  • cooking for others/guests
  • when it's time for the next meal

Personas:

System Persona

(Chef Bot) - the conversational partner created to interact with the users. Helps maintain a certain tone when writing dialog.
  1. Adjectives: foodie, friendly, professional, helpful, knowledgeable
  2. Character: A creative cook that loves to experiment in the kitchen and try out new recipes that are both healthy and tasty. A professional, but not afraid to bend the rules a little. Doesn’t like to dilly dally, but what he lacks in small talk he more than makes up for it with a bit of humour. Worked with a lot of master chefs across the globe. Attempted to start own restaurant but preferred the direct contact with customers via chat. Enjoys spending free time watching the food network.

User Persona

Anna, is a 27 year old entrepreneur who lives in a small city. As her income is unstable she shares an apartment with a roommate who never cooks and has a very demanding job. Anna’s second passion, aside from her business, is cooking and sometimes cooks for her family and friends on weekends and at parties. The Food Network is one of her favourite channels on TV, and she also has a taste for the finer things in life.

  1. Goals: because Anna is such a good friend, she always tries to cook for her roommate when they come home late from work. She has more time to do this because she is her own boss. But since her family has several diabetics and hypertension patients, she wants to cook only healthy meals because she would like to live for a very long time. She is always aware of what her body needs to stay healthy. And when her friends come over she would like to cook for them new and exciting meals. She is one of the lone rangers who like to experiment in the kitchen.
  2. Context: Anna is in the cab ride home thinking of what to cook tonight given what she already has in the fridge.

Crtitical User Journey:

Anna has been craving for some chicken all day and can’t afford to go to a restaurant for a fancy gourmet dinner. Since she has the skills and time, she decides to cook for herself something tasty. She gets suggestions on chicken recipes. Also, since she is health conscious, she only wants low-carb recipes. She gets recipe suggestions with low-carbs. She identifies the ingredients she already has at home and adds the remainders to her shopping list. She saves the recipe and heads to the supermarket near her apartment and buys the remaining ingredients.
When she is finally in her kitchen, she opens the saved recipe and begins to cook. Bon appetite!

User Interface Design

The objective of the interface was to be simple, fresh, ingenious, and in tune with the system persona mentioned above. This was accomplished by implementing a clean interface, with simple shapes and a few innovative details.

Users are welcomed by Chef Bot with a chat interface. The welcome message explains what Chef Bot can do for you right a way. All other features are hidden so you can focus on the main task which is to get recipe suggestions.

When a recipe is selected from the chat interface, the recipe card is expanded to reveal more details like ingredients and a link to instructions. The card expands from within the chat interface so that you can easily move on to other recipe suggestions without having to move back and forth between screens.

The shopping list saves ingredients that you may be interested in purchasing later and can be accessed from the side navigation drawer, revealed by swiping to the right on the device. They are categorized according to the recipe they were selected from. When you done with a recipe, you can delete all of its ingredients easily by just swiping to the left.

For users who want to search for recipes on their own without the help of Chef Bot, you can access search on the home screen in the top right corner. The results can also be filtered according to your needs.

Recipes you would like to re-visit can easily be accessed from 'Favorites' - a menu item also accessed from the side navigation drawer. Recipes can be saved when you are looking a detailed recipe card from the options menu.

For the health conscious user, you can set your preferred diet in settings, which is also accessed from the side navigation drawer. It contains all the most common diet preferences. Other less common preferences can be used for filtering while chatting with Chef Bot.

Interaction Design

The interaction model was mainly inspired by Material Design, and because two of Chef Bot's personality traits are friendly and professional, I chose to to add a few subtle but fun animations to enhance the experience.
Now remember one of the goals of this project is to demonstrate my design and development skills, so I did things a bit differently. In order to test the interaction styles, I actually built the app myself using React Native instead of high fidelity prototypes. I wanted to see if my ideas were both feasible with the actual data and on the given platform. My first platform to test on was Android.

The navigation between the four main screens (home, favorites, shopping list & settings) uses a side navigation drawer which can be accessed by swiping right on the device. Similar to that seen in the Gmail android app.
PS. Jury is still out on whether or not to add sign in and user profile, for now they are not necessary.

On the home/chat interface, the goal was to minimize content given to the user and limit interactions that involve moving to another screen so that the user does not lose track of where they are in the app.
For example, when recipes are requested only three cards are displayed from an array of thousands of options. So instead of endless scrolling, if you need more options you can just type 'more' and the bot will display more options. The cards slide in to indicate that you can scroll down for more options if some of them are hidden from view.
To view more details about a recipe, you don't have to leave the current screen. The card will flip 'onTap' and expand to show more details. Tap 'X' to close the card and it will flip and shrink back to it's original position, so you can quickly go back to deciding what to cook.
Also, originally I had added an options menu to the card but to avoid congestion on the screen I chose to just add the 'favorite' icon only so you can just tap it to add to favorites.
